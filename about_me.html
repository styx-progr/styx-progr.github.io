What have I done so far?

Born on 16.08.1996 in Regensburg I started going to 'Grundschule Pr√ºfening' in 2003. From 2007 until 2015 I went to 'Privat-Gymnasium PINDL'. Then I started studying the same year at the university Regensburg doing my bachelor's degree in biology. For my bachelor's thesis "Third generation sequencing of two Leucanthemum chloroplast genomes using Oxford Nanopore MinION" I sequenced two chloroplast genomes with the MinION. During this thesis I had to extract DNA, enrich it using PCR, and then purifying it prior to sequencing. After sequencing we assembled the chloroplast genomes with the standard bioinformatics tools available. With a master's student doing the same workflow but using Illumina for sequencing, we also compared which method would be better, as this was a first try to introduce these procedures to the chair (evolution and systematic of plants). This work contributed towards a publication. During this time I got help a few times from a bioinformatician that worked at the same chair. As I saw him doing his "magic" I decided that I also want to study bioinformatics for my master's.

With finishing my bachelor's in march of 2019 I immediately started my master's in april at the Friedrich Schiller university Jena to learn the "magic". So far I haven't officialy finished my master's but I already handed in my thesis in march and just waiting for my certificate. For my thesis "Predicting RNA modifications with logistic regression and random forests using summary statistics" as the title already says I trained classifiers using two different methods: logistic regression and random forests (as an example of linear and non-linear classifiers). These classifiers were trained to predict RNA modifications based on summary statistics that were calculated on the sequencing signal of a MinION. This was done as the "modifications" group of the chair (RNA Bioinformatics & High-Throughput Analysis) wanted to know whether simpler models are already sufficient enough to predict modifications.
